RAIN ON THE ROOF

[SOURCES]
In the poem, “Rain on the Roof", what does the poet attach the darkness with?

(A) Joyful mood

(B) ** Sadness

(C) Pleasant

(D) Soothing

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -1 Answer. The poet attaches the darkness with sadness. According to him, darkness has melancholy and is gently weeping in rainy tears.

Concept note-2: -According to the poet, what is nature’s mood in ‘rainy darkness’? The poet shows the darkness to be in a melancholy or sad mood. This sadness is further heightened when the poet shows the darkness to be weeping and shedding tears in the form of raindrops.

Concept note-3: -The poet calls darkness melancholy because it makes him sad. Was this answer helpful?

Concept note-4: -Answer: “Melancholy darkness” refers to the dark rain bearing clouds. The poet imagines that the clouds covering the sky are gloomy and depressed because they are heavy and grey. The poet further imagines that the clouds are weeping and their tears are falling down as rain drops.

Concept note-5: -The darkness of the night and the sound of the rain makes him feel sad and reflective, which is why the poet refers to it as melancholic.
