CLASS 9

THE LOST CHILD

[SOURCES]
The rescuer of the lost child took him to several stalls to

(A) Buy things for him

(B) reunite the child to his parents

(C) make him forget his parents

(D) ** make him stop crying first

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Answer: The man in the shrine rescued the lost child. He lifted him up in his arms and tried to soothe him. He took him to the nearest roundabout swing and offered to buy him flowers, balloons and sheets.

Concept note-2: -3. How did the man try to quieten the crying lost child? Answer: He first took him to the roundabout, then to the snake-charmer, next the balloon seller, after that to the flower-seller and finally to the sweetmeat seller, hoping to quieten the crying lost child.

Concept note-3: -Ans: The man who had rescued the child had a heart full of goodness. He did not ignore the painful shrieks of the boy calling for his parents. Risking his own safety, he bent down in the crowd to rescue the child from getting trampled.

Concept note-4: -The stranger was a kind-hearted and concerned person. He picked up the lost child to save him from getting trampled at the entrance of the shrine. When he realised that the child was separated from his parents, he tried to soothe him by offering him many attractive things at the fair: be it sweets or rides on swings.
