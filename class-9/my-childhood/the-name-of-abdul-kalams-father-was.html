the name of Abdul kalam’s father was

(A) Samsuddin

(B) vasitiddim

(C) ** Jainulabdeen

(D) none of these

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -His father Jainulabdeen Marakayar was a boat owner and imam of a local mosque; his mother Ashiamma was a housewife. His father owned a ferry that took Hindu pilgrims back and forth between Rameswaram and the now uninhabited Dhanushkodi. Kalam was the youngest of four brothers and one sister in his family.

Concept note-2: -His father Jainulabdeen was not much educated, wasn’t rich but was a generous, wise, and simple man. He was very strict and severe. His mother Ashiamma was a generous lady and used to feed many people in their home. Kalam’s family respected all religions.

Concept note-3: -Solution : Abdul Kalam’s father, Jainulabdin, was a tall and handsome man. Although he did not have much formal education, he was progressive and valued education. He was an austere man and didn’t have much wealth, however, he was a generous man and provided both material and emotional security to his family.

Concept note-4: -Kalam’s father was a simple self-disciplined man. He neither had formal education nor much wealth and he avoided all inessential comforts and luxuries.
