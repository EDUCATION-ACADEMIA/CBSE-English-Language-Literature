Why did Kalam collect tamarind seeds?

(A) to grow plants

(B) to eat them

(C) ** to earn good money

(D) none

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Kalam collected tamarind seeds. (b) Why did he collect these seeds? Answer: These seeds were collected by Kalam as they were in great demand in the market during the Second World War and could be sold easily for a good sum of money.

Concept note-2: -2. Why did Abdul collect tamarind seeds in 1939? Kalam collected tamarind seeds in 1939 because he wanted to study the effects of radiation on plants.

Concept note-3: -Abdul Kalam used to collect the tamarind seeds and used to sell them to provision shop on the Mosque street in Rameshwaram. A days collection would fetch him the princely one anna.

Concept note-4: -Answer. Explanation: He got one anna (about six paise) for a day’s collection.

Concept note-5: -Abdul Kalam was eight years old when the Second World War broke out. Suddenly, there was a great demand for tamarind seeds. He would collect those seeds and sell them in the market. He got one anna (about six paise) for a day’s collection.
