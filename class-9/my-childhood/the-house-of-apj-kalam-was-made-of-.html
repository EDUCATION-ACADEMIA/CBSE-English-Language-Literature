The house of A.P.J. Kalam was made of ____

(A) Bricks and Cement

(B) Bricks and Mud

(C) Stone and Limestone

(D) ** Bricks and Limestone

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -APJ Abdul Kalam’s ancestral house was very old. It was built in the nineteenth century. It was a pucca house, which was made of bricks and limestone. It is located on the Mosque Street in Rameswaram.

Concept note-2: -Solution : Abdul Kalam’s ancestral house was situated on Mosque Street in Rameshwaram. It had been built in the middle of the nineteenth century and was a fairly large, pucca house made of limestone and brick.

Concept note-3: -Kalam has so simple live. he lived in his ancestral house, which was built in the middle of 19th century. It was not fairly large and pucca house, made of limestone and brick, on the Mosque street in Rameswaram.

Concept note-4: -About Abdul Kalam house This humble abode in Rameswaram’s Mosque street is the place where one of India’s greatest presidents spent his early days. He lived with his parents, Jainulabdeen and Ashiamma and his brother. Abdul Kalam’s childhood house was converted into a Museum in 2011.
