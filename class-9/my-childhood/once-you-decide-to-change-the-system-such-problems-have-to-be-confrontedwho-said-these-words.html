“Once you decide to change the system, such problems have to be confronted."Who said these words?

(A) Kalam’s father

(B) Lakshmana sastry

(C) ** Sivasubramania Iyer

(D) Ramanadh Sastry

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -“Once you decide to change the system, such problems have to be confronted.” What system is being referred to in the sentence from the chapter “My Childhood”? What are such problems? Hint: Dr A.P.J.

Concept note-2: -Dear Student, Sivasubramania was referring to the system of discriminating people on the basis of religion. Such problems include the narrow-mindedness of people, poison of social inequality and communal intolerance.

Concept note-3: -Answer: Answer: Sivasubramania Iyer taught Kalam to confront prejudice. He told him that if he wanted to bring about a change, such problems needed to be confronted.

Concept note-4: -The system of religious discrimination is being referred to in this sentence and the problem of a boycott on the basis of one’s faith is obvious to be encountered while attempting to bring about a change. Explanation: The chapter titled ‘My Childhood’ discusses the autobiographical account of former president A.P.J.
