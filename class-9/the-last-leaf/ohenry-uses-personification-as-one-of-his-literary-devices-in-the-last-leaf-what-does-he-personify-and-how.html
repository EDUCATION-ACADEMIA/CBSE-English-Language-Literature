O.Henry uses personification as one of his literary devices in ‘’The Last Leaf”. What does he personify, and how?

(A) The leaf, as ‘’Madame Leaf”

(B) ** Pneumonia, as ‘’Mr. Pneumonia”

(C) A tree, as ‘’Mrs. Tree”

(D) The artist, as ‘’the leaf”

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Henry, pneumonia was personified as a human. It was even called “Mr. Pneumonia” in the story. Pneumonia was introduced as a cold stranger, “Toward winter a cold stranger entered Greenwich Village".

Concept note-2: -Answer and Explanation: In the story, “The Last Leaf", pneumonia was personified as an invisible antagonist. An antagonist is the counterpart of the main character who brings conflicts in the story. The illness brought a scare to the city of Washington, which eventually visited the main character, Johnsy, in the story.

Concept note-3: -based on the authors description mr. pneumonia can be compared to the grim reaper which is the personification of death in the story, pneumonia acts like the grim reaper jhonsy discouraged. by the turn her life has taken tells sue that she will soon die when the last leaf off the vine outside her window.

Concept note-4: -These images make the reader call to mind a ruthless and revolting old man who wheezes, who is cold at heart, and who has fingers made of ice. By personifying the disease, O’Henry makes Johnsy’s opponent seem real and makes the reader want to rally behind Johnsy in her fight against pneumonia.
