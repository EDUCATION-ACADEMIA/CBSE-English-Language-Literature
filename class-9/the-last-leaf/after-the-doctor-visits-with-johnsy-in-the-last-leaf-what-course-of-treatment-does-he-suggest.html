CLASS 9

THE LAST LEAF

[SOURCES]
After the doctor visits with Johnsy in ‘’The Last Leaf”, what course of treatment does he suggest?

(A) He recommends strict bed-rest.

(B) He recommends sitting outside next to the trees.

(C) ** He recommends that she take an interest in a man or in the new winter fashions.

(D) He recommends that she enter hospice care.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -After the doctor visits with Johnsy in ‘’The Last Leaf”, what course of treatment does he suggest? He recommends strict bed-rest.

Concept note-2: -The doctor said “Johnsy, it seems, has made up her mind that she is not going to get well. If she doesn’t want to live, medicines will not help her.” Sue tried her best to make Johnsy take an interest in things around her. She talked about clothes and fashions, but Johnsy did not respond.

Concept note-3: -She would sit beside Johnsy’s bed and paint so that she could earn for herself and Johnsy. She comforted Johnsy and dealt with her cranky behaviour very patiently. With her cheerful disposition and optimism, she tried her best to reassure Johnsy that the old ivy leaves had nothing to do with her illness.

Concept note-4: -“The Last Leaf” concerns Johnsy, a poor young woman who is seriously ill with pneumonia. She believes that when the ivy vine on the wall outside her window loses all its leaves, she will also die. Her neighbour Behrman, an artist, tricks her by painting a leaf on the wall.

Concept note-5: -Which of the following would the doctor most likely agree with? A patient must believe they will get better, and this will help his or her chance of recovery. Why does Johnsy count the leaves? She compares herself to the falling leaves; she believes that when she counts none left, she will die.
