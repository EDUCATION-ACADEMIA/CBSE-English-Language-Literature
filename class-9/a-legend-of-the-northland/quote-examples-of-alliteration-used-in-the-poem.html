Quote examples of Alliteration used in the poem.

(A) tell the tales to you

(B) faint with fasting

(C) cakes that seem too small

(D) ** all

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Alliteration: It is the repetition of the consonant sounds in a line of a poem. e.g. “my mother”, “that thought”, “I said was, see you soon”. 1 Crore+ students have signed up on EduRev.

Concept note-2: -"Be a worthy worker and work will come.” “Billions of bilious blue blistering barnacles!” “Who are wise in love, love most, say least.” “When the rich wage war, it’s the poor who die."

Concept note-3: -Repetition: The word smile has been used thrice: All I did was smile and smile and smile. Alliteration: Repetition of the letter s: But all I said was see you soon, Amma.

Concept note-4: -The repetition of initial stressed, consonant sounds in a series of words within a phrase or verse line. Alliteration need not reuse all initial consonants; “pizza” and “place” alliterate. Example: “With swift, slow; sweet, sour; adazzle, dim” from Gerard Manley Hopkins’s “Pied Beauty.” Browse poems with alliteration.
