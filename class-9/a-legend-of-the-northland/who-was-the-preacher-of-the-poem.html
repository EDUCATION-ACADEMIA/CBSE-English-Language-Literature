Who was the preacher of the poem?

(A) ** Saint Peter

(B) Saint John

(C) Saint Jesus

(D) Saint Bishop

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Answer: Saint Peter was an apostle of Jesus Christ. He travelled around the land, preaching the message of Christ. During the course of his journey, sometimes, he did not get food and water.

Concept note-2: -Saint Peter was a wise saint. He had travelled and preached a lot so he was tired and hungry. He had arrived at her door for some food. Still the lady made him wait for the cake for a long time.

Concept note-3: -He was crucified in 64 or 67 AD during a period of major persecution by Emperor Nero. Originally named Simon, the Gospel of Mark tells us that Peter received his new name from Jesus as a sign of his role in the construction of the future Church: “You are Peter, and on this rock I will build my church” (Mt 16:17-19).

Concept note-4: -The poem ‘A legend of the Northland’ is a folktale that is passed from one generation to another in Northland. It is a story that revolves around Saint Peter and the little woman who was punished for her selfishness.

Concept note-5: -Expert-Verified Answer Saint Peter was a holy man. He used to preach people. He often made long journeys for his preaching purpose.
