CLASS 9

WIND

[SOURCES]
the speaker does not want the wind to break the

(A) doors and windows

(B) ** shutter’s of windows

(C) shutter’s of doors

(D) none of the above

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Answer: The wind breaks the shutters of the windows, scatters the papers, throws down the books on the shelf, tears the pages of the books and brings the rain.

Concept note-2: -Complete answer: The poet asks the wind to come slowly and softly but not quickly in a powerful manner and he also asks it not to tear pages of books, break shutters of windows, and not to scatter the pages.

Concept note-3: -Answer: The poet wants the wind to behave softly and peacefully, without breaking his shutters, without scattering the papers and without throwing down the books on the shelf.
