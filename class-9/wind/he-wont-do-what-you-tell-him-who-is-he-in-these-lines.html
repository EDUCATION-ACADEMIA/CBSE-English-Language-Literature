“He won’t do what you tell him “ who is he in these lines?

(A) The Author

(B) The children

(C) ** Wind

(D) God

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -It means that the wind won’t listen to us and do what it wished to do. Explanation: The given verse is taken from the poem ‘Wind’ written by Subramania Bharti. The poem speaks about the wind, which is symbolized as problems and difficulties faced in life, and how to remain strong during those times.

Concept note-2: -He in the above lines refers to the wind. (b) What is he being told to do? Answer: He is being told to blow softly and not break the shutters of the windows, scatter the papers or throw down the books from the shelves.

Concept note-3: -What do the last four lines of the poem mean to you? Answer: In the last four lines, the poet inspires us to be determined and face all the hardships or obstacles coming in life courageously. He tells us that the wind can only extinguish the weak fires but intensifies the stronger ones.

Concept note-4: -Answer: The poet wants us to realise the true nature and power of the wind. He advises us to build strong homes. The doors must be closed firmly.
