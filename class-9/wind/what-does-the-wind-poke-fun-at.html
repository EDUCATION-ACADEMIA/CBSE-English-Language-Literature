What does the wind poke fun at?

(A) Weak houses and weak doors.

(B) Weak rafters and weak wood.

(C) Weak hearts and weak bodies.

(D) ** Any living or non-living thing that is weak and crumbling.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Answers. In the poem “The Wind” The wind pokes fun at any living or non-living thing that is weak and crumbling.-The poem gives an important message that we should be mentally tough and physically strong in order to survive the hardships of life.-A weak person breaks down like a weak building and crumbles.

Concept note-2: -What does the wind poke fun at? (a) Weak houses and weak doors.

Concept note-3: -The wind pokes fun at weaklings by crumbling their lives, crumbling their houses, rafters, and causing devastation.It winnows the weaklings out of the the strong ones and crushes them all.

Concept note-4: -How does wind make fun of weaklings? Answer: Wind makes fun of the weaklings by separating them from the strong and then crushing them by its force. Wind who is very strong does not behave in a friendly way with the weak.
