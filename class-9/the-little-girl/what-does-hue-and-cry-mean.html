What does ‘hue and cry’ mean?

(A) a nice conversation

(B) an argument

(C) ** angry protest

(D) a meal

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Britannica Dictionary definition of HUE AND CRY. [singular] : an angry protest about something. There was a hue and cry in opposition to the film.

Concept note-2: -hue and cry • -und-KRYE • noun. 1 a : a loud outcry formerly used in the pursuit of one who is suspected of a crime b : the pursuit of a suspect or a written proclamation for the capture of a suspect 2 : a clamor of protest 3 : hubbub.

Concept note-3: -Detailed Solution. The correct answer is ‘Angry protest. ‘ The idiom ‘Hue and cry’ means a noisy expression of public anger or disapproval.

Concept note-4: -a noisy expression of public anger or disapproval: There has been a great hue and cry about the council’s plans to close the school.

Concept note-5: -synonyms for hue and cry Compare Synonyms. brouhaha. outcry. uproar. bugle call.
