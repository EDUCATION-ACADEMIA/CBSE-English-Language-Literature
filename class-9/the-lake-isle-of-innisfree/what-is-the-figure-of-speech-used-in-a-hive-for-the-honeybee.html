What is the figure of speech used in “A hive for the honeybee?"

(A) ** Alliteration

(B) Repetition

(C) Metaphor

(D) Simile

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Alliteration-It is the repetition of a letter at the start of closely placed words. The repetition of the letter: ‘H’ in have there, a hive for the honeybee.

Concept note-2: -The boy buzzed around as busy as a bee.

Concept note-3: -The honeybee hive is a powerful metaphor for all communities of people and the issues they face together in their shared ecosystems.

Concept note-4: -2 Answers. A simile is figure of speech which involves a comparison between unlike things. The student is as busy as a bee. As busy as a bee is also a simile.

Concept note-5: -HONEYBEE (noun) definition and synonyms | Macmillan Dictionary.
