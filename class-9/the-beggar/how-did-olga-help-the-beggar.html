How did Olga help the beggar?

(A) by giving him food

(B) giving him medicine

(C) ** by chopping wood for him

(D) none

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Lushkoff was a drunkard and used to beg . however, his life changed when he met Sergei. she taught him the value of self respect and leading a noble life by working hard. Olga was the cook of Sergei who also helped Lushkoff.

Concept note-2: -She expresses sympathy and kindness towards him. She used to chop the wood for Lushkoff. It is her words and kind deeds that inspires Lushkoff to change. Thus, Olga saves Lushkoff.

Concept note-3: -Answer: She glanced at the beggar with anger, shoved him aside with her elbow, unlocked the shed and angrily banged the door. She then flung down an axe at his feet, spat angrily and appeared to be scolding him.

Concept note-4: -Ans. Olga, Sergei’s seemingly ill-tempered cook, had been the main motivating factor behind Lushkoff’s positive change. She saved him by rousing the positivity in him that had got suppressed due to his alcoholism. She scolded him, cursed him but also shed tears for him and suffered misery for his sake.

Concept note-5: -Ans. Sergei refused to give alms to Lushkolf, the beggar, instead offered to give him work. He took him home and gave him the work of chopping wood.
