What was Lucy doing after her death?

(A) coming to poet’s dreams

(B) was disturbing

(C) ** moving here and there in the in the earth’s course of rotation

(D) all

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -As he comes to terms of her death, the poet feels that in her death his beloved Lucy has become a part of Nature. She is now under the surface of the earth and revolving along with it on its path. He tells us that like other stones, rocks and trees she also revolves with the earth now.

Concept note-2: -Lucy, after her death, has part of Nature as she has mingled with the soil. As such she is a part of the other things on the earth like rocks, stones or trees. She has now become a part of Nature.

Concept note-3: -In the first stanza, the poet says that the death of his beloved made him very depressed. He says that his beloved has now become a non-living thing which cannot feel the touch of anything on the earth.

Concept note-4: -The poet was very sad on his beloved’s death. He was shocked. But the sleep brought peace to his mind. He realised that his beloved had become part of nature and would always remain around him.
