Why does it takes much time to kill tree?

(A) Because the axe is not sharp

(B) Because the wood cutter is not well

(C) Because the tree is in standing position

(D) ** Because, by consuming the Earth, the tree is very strong

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -It is not an easy job to kill a tree, as it has grown strong by absorbing many elements for many years. It has taken nutrients from the soil and absorbed sunlight, air and water for a long time such that many leaves had grown on it.

Concept note-2: -1. (a) Ans-It takes much time to kill a tree because it grows slowly and rises of the earth by absorbing years of sunlight, air and water.

Concept note-3: -A tree takes many years to grow to its full size by absorbing sunlight, air and water. The roots of the tree grow deep and hold it firmly in the soil. So, only a chop cannot kill the tree because it will slowly heal up and grow to its original shape and size.

Concept note-4: -Over the years, the tree has grown strong and hence, it will take much time to kill a tree.

Concept note-5: -The tree is finally killed when its roots are uprooted and it scorches and chokes in sunlight and air. This process leads to the browning, hardening, twisting and thereby, withering of the roots.
