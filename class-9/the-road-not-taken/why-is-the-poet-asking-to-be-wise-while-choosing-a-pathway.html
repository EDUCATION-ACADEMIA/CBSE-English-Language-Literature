THE ROAD NOT TAKEN

[SOURCES]
Why is the poet asking to be wise while choosing a pathway?

(A) ** because there is no Going Back option

(B) Because it is the only one road

(C) because it is one sided road

(D) None

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Answer. Answer: The poet is asked to be wise while choosing the path in the lesson “THE ROAD NOT TAKEN” by ROBERT FROST because one could not alter or change the path after choosing one.

Concept note-2: -Explanation: The poet looked down the road as far as he could see because the road he was walking on that morning got diverged into two; he was in doubt as to which road he should continue walking. One of them was quite worn out; the other was grassy. The poet was making up his mind.

Concept note-3: -Answer. Explanation: The poet is pragmatic enough to know he may not be able to retrace his steps as one way leads to another. The path he has chosen will probably take him so far from the trodden path that he may find it difficult to return.

Concept note-4: -Answer: yes the poet made the right choice as this decision bought change in his life and he was happy about his decision.
