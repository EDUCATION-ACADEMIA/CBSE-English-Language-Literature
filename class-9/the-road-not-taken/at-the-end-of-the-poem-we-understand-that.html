CLASS 9

THE ROAD NOT TAKEN

[SOURCES]
At the end of the poem, we understand that

(A) the traveler ia happy about his choice

(B) the traveler regrets his choice

(C) ** the traveler doesn’t give a clear answer about his choice

(D) the traveler is sad about his choice

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Avoiding the popular rat-race, he opted for the option that broke fresh grounds. Was this answer helpful?

Concept note-2: -The speaker, when trying to choose which road to take, looks for the road that seems less worn. At the end of the poem, the speaker asserts that choosing the road less traveled “has made all the difference"-the suggestion being that he or she has led a life of nonconformity, and is happier because of it.

Concept note-3: -We see this in the last lines of the poem, which read: I took the one less traveled by, And that has made all the difference. Essentially, the speaker is saying that later in life he will look back in time and see that moment as one of great significance.

Concept note-4: -The last two lines of the poem means that the poet has taken the road which was not used by a number of people as it was the road of hardwork which would be leading him to success.
