What was the colour of the woods?

(A) Green

(B) Blue

(C) Red

(D) ** Yellow

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The poet has described the wood as yellow which basically means that the leaves have turned yellow in color-that is it is the season of autumn.

Concept note-2: -But what does that yellow wood symbolise? Well, most obviously, the yellow wood in Frost’s poem symbolises autumn: the wood is yellow because the leaves – once green during the fulness of spring and summer – have turned yellow as summer has given way to autumn.

Concept note-3: -Answer: (i) Yellow woods refers to the forest in the autumn season. (ii) The road was full of grass and very few people had used it. It seemed to invite people to tread on it.

Concept note-4: -noun. yel·low·wood ˈye-lō-ˌwu̇d. ˈye-lə-: any of various trees having yellowish wood or yielding a yellow extract. especially : a leguminous tree (Cladrastis lutea) of the southern U.S. having showy white fragrant flowers and yielding a yellow dye.

Concept note-5: -The wood was looking yellowish because of yellow leaves of the trees. ‘Two roads’ implies that the poet has to choose one destination.
