The word ‘undergrowth’ means low-growing trees and bushes of a forest?

(A) ** True

(B) False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -undergrowth. noun. un·der·growth ˈən-dər-ˌgrōth. : low growth on the floor of a forest including seedlings and saplings, shrubs, and herbs.

Concept note-2: -noun. 1. low-lying vegetation or small trees growing beneath larger trees; underbrush. 2. the condition of being undergrown or undersized.

Concept note-3: -a mass of bushes, small trees, and plants, especially growing under the trees in a forest: Police discovered the body hidden in thick undergrowth.

Concept note-4: -In “The Road Not Taken, ” undergrowth refers to the low-growing plants that cover the forest floor.
