CLASS 9

KATHMANDU

[SOURCES]
The narrator is in a dilemma about:

(A) ** the route to his home

(B) the means of transport

(C) the day of destination

(D) all of these

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The dilemma is that the speaker can only take one road. While the speaker would like to take both roads, he can only take one.

Concept note-2: -Answers. The poet’s dilemma was that he did not know which road he had to choose when the road split into two. He looked down to one road and realised that, that road was taken by most of the people.

Concept note-3: -Answer: The traveller finds himself in the yellow woods at a point where the two roads diverge. The problem that he faces is that he cannot decide which road to take to continue his journey.

Concept note-4: -In the poem, “The Road Not Taken” by Robert Frost, the speaker is in dilemma on choosing a right road to travel. He has two alternatives, but does not know where they would lead. He is in confusion and finally makes up his mind to choose a road which had been walked by some others.
