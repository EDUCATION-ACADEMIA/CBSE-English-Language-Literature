Which river flows through Kathmandu?

(A) Kosi

(B) ** Bagmati

(C) Gandak

(D) Khalsa

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Bagmati River flows through the Kathmandu valley of Nepal, separating the cities of Kathmandu from Patan, before flowing through Madesh Province of southern Nepal and joins the Kamla River in the Indian state of Bihar. It is considered holy by both Hindus and Buddhists.

Concept note-2: -Kathmandu is dissected by eight rivers, the main river of the valley, the Bagmati and its tributaries, of which the Bishnumati, Dhobi Khola, Manohara Khola, Hanumante Khola, and Tukucha Khola are predominant.
