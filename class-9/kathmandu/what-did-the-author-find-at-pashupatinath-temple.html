What did the author find at Pashupatinath temple?

(A) Chappiness

(B) peace

(C) ** chaos and noise

(D) All

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Answer: The atmosphere at the Pashupatinath temple was full of chaos and confusion. Priests, hawkers, devotees, tourists, cows, monkeys, pigeons and dogs roamed through the grounds.

Concept note-2: -Answer: The author says that the atmosphere in Pashupatinath temple was an atmosphere of ‘febrile confusion’. He says that priests, hawkers, devotees, tourists, cows, monkeys, pigeons and dogs can be seen roaming through the grounds.

Concept note-3: -In the Pashupatinath Temple, the author noticed that entry was allowed only to the Hindus. This led to chaos between the tourists, pilgrims as well as priests. There were sights of the River Bagmati getting polluted by people washing their clothes, throwing dry flowers and wastes in it, and bathing in the river waters.

Concept note-4: -The general scene outside Pashupatinath temple was one of ‘febrile confusion’. The grounds were filled with humans and animals alike: priests and devotees, monkeys, cows, dogs and pigeons. people were jostling to get the attention of the priests. Way was given to a Nepalese princess as she came to the temple.

Concept note-5: -What does the author mean when he says “At Pashupatinath, there is an atmosphere of febrile confusion”? Text Solution. Solution : The author makes this remark to imply there is hectic and chaotic activity around the temple.
