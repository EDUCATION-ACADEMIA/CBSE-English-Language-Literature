A HOUSE IS NOT A HOME

Who made a collection of money for the author?

(A) ** the students in the new high school

(B) the teachers in the old primary school

(C) neighbours

(D) social workers

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The correct answer is option (a) the students in the new high school. Explanation: The students in the new high school made a collection of money for the author. The author’s house was completely destroyed in the fire.

Concept note-2: -how. The author’s grandmother got the narrator ready for school. She bathed and dressed him for school and fetch his wooden slate which she had already washed and plastered with yellow chalk, an earthen ink-pot and a red pen.

Concept note-3: -Answer: I think it is bibliophile.

Concept note-4: -They all had contributed the things he needed to help him cope with the situation. People introduced themselves to him. They even invited him to their houses.

Concept note-5: -Answer: In the beginning, before her house was burnt, the writer was unhappy in her new school. She felt awkward and lonely starting as a freshman after having been a senior in junior high. She found it difficult to relate to her classmates and teachers, and continued to visit her old teachers, who she missed terribly.
