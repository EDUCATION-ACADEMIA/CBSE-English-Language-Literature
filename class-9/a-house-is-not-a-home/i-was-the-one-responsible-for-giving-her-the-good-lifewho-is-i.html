A HOUSE IS NOT A HOME

[SOURCES]
I was the one responsible for giving her ‘the good life’Who is ‘I’?

(A) ** Zan

(B) the cat

(C) Zan’s mother

(D) no one

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -So cat knew that the author was responsible for giving it the good life .. Also the author was very fond of his cat . He would poke the cat in the morning who would then tag along him and cuddle up in his arms, pocket of robe, his lap or wherever it found it to be comfortable ..

Concept note-2: -Zan says that he is also back since he wants to point out the return of his faith in the goodness of life. He had drifted away from joys of friendship and comfort of familiarity on moving to a new school. By the time his cat returned, his gratitude for life had also returned in the company of new friends.

Concept note-3: -Ans: ‘Good Life’, according to the speaker, Zan, means a life where the pet cat was fed well, pampered, and taken care of in every way.

Concept note-4: -Zan is a teenager who is struggling with the changes in his life. He feels awkward, strange, isolated and embarrassed like any other teenager. Little matters gain massive proportion for him. However, he is very alert as he notices the fire as soon as it begins and he rushes out to call the firefighters.
