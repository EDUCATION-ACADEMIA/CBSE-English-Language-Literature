[SOURCES]
Who used to wake up the cat?

(A) Zan’s mother

(B) Zan’s aunt

(C) ** Zan

(D) None

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Answer. Answer: while doing his homework he saw fire broke out.

Concept note-2: -At this time, Zan realized that his cat was not to be seen anywhere. He realised this horror and broke down in tears thinking about his losses – his school, his house, his cat. The firemen did not allow them to go back into the house for the night as it was no longer safe.

Concept note-3: -So cat knew that the author was responsible for giving it the good life .. Also the author was very fond of his cat . He would poke the cat in the morning who would then tag along him and cuddle up in his arms, pocket of robe, his lap or wherever it found it to be comfortable ..

Concept note-4: -01 A House Is Not a Home This story reflects the challenges of being a teenager, and the problems of growing up. The author drives home the message that life without affection is useless. Her feelings of depression and insecurity were natural when she thought that she had lost everything.
